welcome to horror classics

Horror classics are foundational films and literary works that established the tropes, monsters, and psychological dread that define the genre today. From the Gothic terror of the Universal Monsters to the modern, reality-bending terrors of the late 20th century, these stories set the gold standard for fright.

Nosferatu (1922): The unauthorized adaptation of Dracula and a silent-era German Expressionist masterpiece that perfected the use of shadow and silhouette horror.

The Universal Monsters (1930s): This era created the cinematic horror playbook with Dracula (1931) (starring Bela Lugosi) and Frankenstein (1931).

The Slasher & Psychological Boom (1960s-1970s): Alfred Hitchcock's Psycho (1960) redefined the genre by bringing terror into everyday motels, while Night of the Living Dead (1968) established the modern zombie mythos. Halloween (1978) and The Exorcist (1973) later cemented modern cinematic fear.
